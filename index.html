🗣️ Rule: say it yourself!

Present Simple is for facts, habits, and things that are always true.
We use it with always, usually, often, sometimes, never, every day.
Negative: don't/doesn't + V  |  Question: Do/Does + subject + V?

Present Continuous is for actions happening right now.
We use it with now, at the moment, today, look!, listen!, right now.
Negative: am/is/are + not + Ving  |  Question: Am/Is/Are + subject + Ving?

📖 Adverb Rules (click to show/hide) ▼

Adverbs of frequency (always, usually, often, sometimes, never):
➜ After to be (am/is/are): The city is always noisy.
➜ Before other verbs: People usually walk fast.

Time expressions (now, at the moment, every day):
➜ Usually at the end: The cars are moving now.

Reading: Moscow — A City of Contrasts

Moscow is the capital of Russia. It is one of the biggest cities in Europe. More than 12 million people live here.

Every day, Moscow is full of life. The streets are always crowded with people and cars. There is heavy traffic, especially in the morning and in the evening. People usually walk fast because they are in a hurry. The city has got a lot of tall buildings, modern shopping centres, and beautiful old churches.

Look at Red Square now! Tourists are taking photos. Some people are walking near the Kremlin. The bells of the Spasskaya Tower are ringing at the moment.

Moscow has got convenient public transport. You can travel by metro, bus, or tram. The Moscow Metro is very beautiful — some stations look like museums! There are also many parks and green zones. In summer, people often go to Gorky Park or VDNKh. They walk, ride bikes, and enjoy the fresh air.

But life in a big city is not always easy. The cost of living is high. There is constant noise and pollution. Some people feel tired of the hustle and bustle.

However, Moscow never sleeps. At night, the lights shine, and the city is still alive.
